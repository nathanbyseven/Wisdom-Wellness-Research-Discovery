The Sevenstudios Proposal

Option C & Why It Needs Reframing

What Option C Originally Proposed

A Strategic Hybrid Build: Orchestration Now, Ownership Later. Phase 1 would build a dedicated WW mobile app (iOS and Android) as a front-door orchestration layer integrating Ghutte and GoKollab — keeping Ghutte as the system of record and GoKollab as the community engine, while architecting for future full ownership.

The full scope covers research, product strategy, visual identity, UX, app development, integrations, and launch — over approximately eight months, with a total capped budget of around R997,000.

The Option C concept is strategically sound. The issue is not the idea — it is the timing. Full development must be deferred until you are structurally and financially ready to commit to full ownership.

Why It Must Be Reframed Now

Licensing & compliance risk: Deeply embedding or "merging" Ghutte inside a new custom app raises GoHighLevel licensing, compliance, and App Store/Google Play approval concerns that are not trivial to resolve.

Duplication risk: GoKollab is already a white-label mobile app under the GoHighLevel model. A separate WW app that tightly wraps or duplicates it risks confusing your members with two primary apps.

Full ownership cost: To truly own the stack the way you desire, a future app would ultimately need a WW-owned backend replicating large parts of Ghutte's functionality — which is not financially or operationally realistic immediately.

The Right Reframe

In the near term, Option C becomes a strategy, architecture, and design track — not a commitment to immediate full development. The development phases are triggered only when you explicitly decide to invest in full ownership with the requisite budget and team capacity.

Platform Capabilities

GoHighLevel — What It Can & Cannot Do

APIs & Integration Surface

GoHighLevel, the platform behind Ghutte, provides a range of integration options that are important for your future planning:

REST APIs

GoHighLevel exposes REST endpoints (v1 and v2) for contacts, messaging, workflows, calendars, and other CRM entities. Access is controlled via API keys or OAuth tokens, with capabilities varying by plan level.

Webhooks

HighLevel supports webhook triggers and inbound webhooks, allowing external systems to respond to events — such as form submissions or pipeline stage changes — or to initiate actions within GoHighLevel.

Third-Party Orchestration

Ecosystem tools like Zapier and Make are commonly used to connect GoHighLevel to other apps and services, extending functionality without modifying GoHighLevel itself.

The White-Label Mobile Model

GoHighLevel's white-label mobile model is central to your current setup. This is how GoKollab works:

Branded but GHL-Powered

Agencies on the appropriate plan can deploy custom-branded iOS and Android apps under their own name, with their own icon, colours, and store listings — but the underlying functionality remains GoHighLevel.

Shared Credentials

Your members log into the white-label app using the same credentials they use on the GoHighLevel web platform — which is why GoKollab and Ghutte feel like one system to your members.

What this means for you: any future WW-owned app must be clearly differentiated in scope and positioning from the GoKollab white-label app. They cannot both claim to be "the WW app."

The key opportunity: future custom WW systems can integrate at the data level with Ghutte — synchronising contacts, membership states, and key events via APIs and webhooks — without needing to embed or rewrite the Ghutte platform itself.

Honest Assessment

Limitations, Risks & Realistic Possibilities

Limitations & Risks to Understand

Licensing & compliance: Ghutte is a licensed GoHighLevel instance. Any attempt to deeply embed it inside a new custom app or present Ghutte functionality as an entirely separate native platform must respect licensing terms and App Store policies.

Duplication & member confusion: Building a separate WW app that replicates GoKollab's membership and community features now would duplicate effort and risk confusing your members with two primary apps — the opposite of what you want to achieve.

Resource constraints: Without an internal tech team, you would be reliant on external partners for any custom infrastructure. A premature move into full ownership could create operational risk without sufficient internal capacity to manage it.

Budget vs. return: A full custom stack represents a significant investment that has not yet been justified against your current scale and revenue. A phased approach is more prudent.

What Is Realistically Possible

Optimise Ghutte & GoKollab Now

Better funnels, more robust automations, improved payment workflows, and leak-fixing — e.g. subscription sync between your payment gateway and Ghutte — led by ClubSheIs and informed by Sevenstudios' UX strategy.

Define a Clear Front-Door Experience

Even if the underlying systems remain distributed, you can present members and prospects with a single, coherent WW journey — from first contact through to deep community participation.

Design Your Future Owned App Now

Sevenstudios can complete a full product strategy, architecture blueprint, and design system for a future WW-owned app — ready for implementation when you are financially and structurally prepared.

Your Strategic Roadmap

Three Phases to Full Digital Ownership

Now → 12 months
Phase 1 · Focus: Protect & improve revenue while simplifying the user experience
Stabilise & Clarify

Treat GoKollab as your primary member-facing app in the near term, with improved WW branding and navigation aligned to your calm, emotionally safe UX philosophy. Sevenstudios defines a unified "front door" journey — how users move from podcast, Instagram, and website into membership, programs, and events. ClubSheIs closes known leakages (e.g. payment gateway ↔ Ghutte subscription mismatches) and refines automations. Run foundational user research — surveys and interviews — to validate key flows and emotional safety requirements for the future app experience.

12 → 24 months
Phase 2 · Focus: Design a WW-owned app and backend on paper, ready for future implementation
Design the Owned Stack

Sevenstudios completes a detailed product strategy and architecture blueprint covering: app information architecture and navigation; user journeys and state logic for free users, members, and event-only participants; backend services layout for auth, content, programs, events, and analytics; and integration points with Ghutte through GoHighLevel APIs and webhooks. Finalise a mobile design system — typography, colour, components, interaction patterns — that embodies WW's brand and emotional safety standards. Conduct a financial and operational review to determine readiness for a full build.

24 → 36+ months
Phase 3 · Subject to WW readiness — focus: build the owned app, integrate, and manage a careful transition
Build & Transition

Build a WW backend — user management, content services, programs, events, analytics — aligned with the architecture defined in Phase 2. Build iOS and Android apps (e.g. React Native or Flutter) using the design system and UX flows from earlier phases. Integrate with Ghutte via GoHighLevel APIs and webhooks, ensuring data synchronisation for contacts, membership states, and key events. Plan a staged migration strategy: the new WW app initially acts as a superior experience layer while Ghutte continues to power core revenue operations, and only later hands over more logic to the WW backend.

Conclusion

Your Immediate Decisions & Path Forward

Wisdom & Wellness is well positioned to evolve into a coherent, owned digital universe — without sacrificing current performance. The key is sequencing. You have a rich, functioning ecosystem. What you need is clarity on what happens in what order.

Decisions to Make Now
01

Confirm Ghutte & GoKollab as Your Operational Foundation

Confirm formally that Ghutte and GoKollab remain your operational foundation, managed by ClubSheIs. This is not a limitation — it is the right infrastructure for where you are today. Removing ambiguity here protects your revenue and prevents conflicting technical decisions.

02

Position GoKollab as the Official WW Member App Now

Decide to position GoKollab as your current official WW member app, with improved UX and branding guided by Sevenstudios. This closes the gap between what your members experience today and what it should feel like — without waiting for a custom build.

03

Commission the Strategy, Architecture & Design Track

Commission Sevenstudios to complete the strategy, architecture, and design phases of Option C — the "thinking work" — without committing to immediate development. This gives you a complete blueprint that can be implemented by any development partner when the time is right, and that is 100% valuable regardless of when the build happens.

04

Use the Blueprint to Decide When to Build

In the medium to long term, use the resulting blueprints to decide — based on revenue, team capacity, and risk appetite — when to invest in a fully owned app and backend. Proceed with a deliberate build and migration plan that leverages Ghutte's strengths, respects licensing and platform integrity, and ultimately delivers on your vision of complete digital ownership.

This approach balances ambition with realism — protecting your present while carefully constructing your digital future. You will not sacrifice revenue operations for a speculative build, and you will not sacrifice your long-term ownership vision for short-term convenience.
